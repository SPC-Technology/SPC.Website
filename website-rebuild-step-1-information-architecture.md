# Website Rebuild Step 1: Information Architecture

## 1. Website Objective

### What the New Website Should Achieve
The new website should reposition SPC Technology from a general IT company presentation toward a clearer product-led B2B software company narrative, with Phoebus ERP as the central platform. It should help visitors quickly understand what SPC Technology does, who Phoebus ERP is for, and how to start a sales or implementation conversation.

### Primary Conversion Goals
- Generate qualified inbound leads for Phoebus ERP demos and sales enquiries.
- Encourage direct contact for implementation, customization, and migration discussions.
- Guide existing customers to the correct support or helpdesk destination without creating confusion.

### Secondary Goals
- Establish SPC Technology as a credible software company with domain expertise.
- Support evaluation by different stakeholder types, including management, operations, accounting, and IT.
- Provide a clear path for partners, resellers, or business prospects to start a conversation.
- Reduce reliance on ad hoc explanations by making the core offer and product structure easier to understand.

## 2. Target Audiences

### Business Owners / Decision Makers
- Looking for business software that improves visibility, control, and scalability.
- Need confidence in the vendor, product fit, and implementation capability.
- Care most about outcomes, business coverage, and commercial credibility.

### Operations / Accounting Users
- Looking for practical workflow support across finance, inventory, purchasing, sales, reporting, and daily operations.
- Need to understand how the system supports real operational work.
- Care most about usability, process fit, and reporting value.

### IT / Technical Evaluators
- Looking for deployment fit, customization flexibility, integration capability, security posture, and support model.
- Need enough technical clarity to assess feasibility without turning the site into documentation-heavy content.
- Care most about architecture confidence, implementation approach, and maintainability.

### Partners / Prospects
- Includes implementation partners, referral partners, and companies exploring broader collaboration.
- Need a clear explanation of SPC Technology’s product focus, services, and market positioning.
- Care most about commercial fit and who to contact.

## 5. Page-by-Page Purpose

### Home
- Why it exists: Introduces SPC Technology with a stronger product-company message and directs visitors into the most relevant paths quickly.
- What content it should contain: Clear value proposition, Phoebus ERP positioning, core business benefits, short product/module summary, implementation/support credibility, and CTA blocks for demo and contact.
- What action the visitor should take: Request a demo or explore Phoebus ERP.

### Phoebus ERP Overview
- Why it exists: Acts as the main commercial landing page for the product.
- What content it should contain: Product summary, who it is for, major business areas covered, deployment/customization positioning, and links to modules, implementation, and demo request.
- What action the visitor should take: Continue to modules or request a demo.

### Phoebus ERP Modules
- Why it exists: Helps buyers and operational users understand business coverage in a structured way.
- What content it should contain: Module groups such as finance, accounting, inventory, purchasing, sales, reporting, and other relevant functions already supported by current positioning.
- What action the visitor should take: Identify fit and move to demo or contact.

### Phoebus ERP Implementation
- Why it exists: Explains how SPC Technology delivers the product in practice.
- What content it should contain: Implementation approach, project stages, onboarding expectations, training, support, and typical engagement model.
- What action the visitor should take: Start an implementation discussion.

### Phoebus ERP Customization & Integration
- Why it exists: Reassures prospects that the platform can be adapted to operational needs.
- What content it should contain: Custom development capability, integration approach, data migration support, and boundaries between product standardization and custom work.
- What action the visitor should take: Contact sales for a requirements discussion.

### Solutions By Business Function
- Why it exists: Makes the site easier to navigate for users thinking in terms of department needs rather than product architecture.
- What content it should contain: Function-led summaries for finance, accounting, inventory, procurement, sales operations, reporting, and management visibility.
- What action the visitor should take: Move into relevant product content or contact sales.

### Solutions By Industry
- Why it exists: Gives SPC Technology a place to organize sector-specific positioning later without overcomplicating the launch.
- What content it should contain: A small number of realistic industry pages only if evidence and case material exist; otherwise keep this page light at launch.
- What action the visitor should take: Confirm fit and enquire.

### Services Overview
- Why it exists: Clarifies that SPC Technology provides delivery capability around the product, not just software licensing.
- What content it should contain: Implementation, customization, migration, training, and ongoing support summarized in one place.
- What action the visitor should take: Contact SPC Technology about project scope.

### ERP Implementation Service Page
- Why it exists: Supports buyers evaluating vendor delivery capability.
- What content it should contain: Project methodology, stakeholder involvement, timeline expectations, rollout support, and post-go-live assistance.
- What action the visitor should take: Request a consultation.

### Customization & Development Service Page
- Why it exists: Captures enquiries from businesses with non-standard requirements.
- What content it should contain: Types of customization, extension approach, integration mindset, and practical discussion prompts.
- What action the visitor should take: Submit requirements or contact sales.

### Data Migration Service Page
- Why it exists: Addresses a common ERP purchase concern directly.
- What content it should contain: Migration planning, data preparation expectations, legacy system transition support, and project dependencies.
- What action the visitor should take: Start a migration discussion.

### Training & Ongoing Support Service Page
- Why it exists: Shows that adoption and long-term usage are part of the offer.
- What content it should contain: User training, administrator support, support channels, and continuity after implementation.
- What action the visitor should take: Contact SPC Technology or proceed to support resources.

### Support
- Why it exists: Separates customer support journeys from new-business journeys.
- What content it should contain: Clear routing to helpdesk, documentation, customer access links, and support contact options.
- What action the visitor should take: Go to the appropriate support resource.

### Documentation / Knowledge Base
- Why it exists: Provides a future-ready place for practical product help content.
- What content it should contain: Links to existing documentation resources, user guides, FAQs, and onboarding materials when available.
- What action the visitor should take: Self-serve or escalate to support.

### About
- Why it exists: Builds trust without overemphasizing a generic corporate image.
- What content it should contain: SPC Technology overview, product-company evolution, domain expertise, delivery focus, and reasons to choose the company.
- What action the visitor should take: Contact SPC Technology or continue evaluating Phoebus ERP.

### Contact
- Why it exists: Gives all audiences a simple, unambiguous conversion point.
- What content it should contain: Sales enquiry path, general contact details, support direction if needed, and basic qualification prompts.
- What action the visitor should take: Submit an enquiry.

## 6. Content Migration Map

### Company / About Content
- Move general company introduction into About.
- Keep credibility, experience, and business background content, but reduce broad “IT company” messaging unless it directly supports the software and ERP story.
- Move “why choose us” style material into About and selected homepage sections.

### ERP / Product Content
- Consolidate Phoebus ERP content under the Phoebus ERP section.
- Move scattered product descriptions, feature summaries, and business process explanations into Overview, Modules, and Solutions pages.
- Use the homepage only for high-level product positioning, not full product detail.

### Support / Helpdesk / Documentation Links
- Group all customer assistance links under Support.
- Move any current support buttons, remote help, documentation, or portal access into a dedicated support structure.
- Keep support clearly separated from lead-generation pages so new visitors are not sent into customer-only areas.

### Contact / Sales Content
- Consolidate general contact forms, phone/email details, and sales prompts into Contact.
- Keep “Request a Demo” and “Contact Sales” as repeated CTAs across Home, Phoebus ERP, Solutions, and Services.
- Remove duplicated contact prompts from low-priority pages unless they support conversion.

### Implementation / Customization / Services Content
- Move implementation capability, custom software work, integration, migration, and training content into Services and linked product pages.
- Keep service messaging tied to Phoebus ERP adoption and delivery, rather than presenting services as unrelated standalone offers.
- Use service pages to answer buying questions, not to create a broad agency-style service catalog.

## 7. Content Gaps

- A sharper homepage value proposition that explains SPC Technology and Phoebus ERP in one clear statement.
- A definitive Phoebus ERP product overview written for decision makers.
- Structured module descriptions with consistent scope and terminology.
- A documented implementation process suitable for web presentation.
- Clear customization and integration messaging with practical examples.
- Industry-specific positioning, only if supported by real experience and evidence.
- Customer proof points such as case studies, references, or deployment examples.
- A concise “why SPC Technology” narrative that supports trust and differentiation.
- A support content inventory covering helpdesk links, documentation, FAQs, and user access paths.
- A better-qualified contact and demo intake structure.
- Basic legal/footer content such as privacy and terms if the site will collect leads.

## 8. Recommendation

### Minimum Viable Website for Launch Version 1
Launch with a focused structure that supports lead generation and customer routing without overbuilding:

- Home
- Phoebus ERP Overview
- Phoebus ERP Modules
- Services
- Support
- About
- Contact

This version keeps the site small enough for founder-led execution while making the strategic shift clear: SPC Technology is presented primarily as the company behind Phoebus ERP, with implementation and support capability around the platform. Solutions by industry, deep documentation, and broader proof content can be added in later phases once the core messaging and conversion paths are stable.
